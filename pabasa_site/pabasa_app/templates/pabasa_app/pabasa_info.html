Frequently Asked Questions

To create an account on PABASA, visit the signup page and select whether you're a teacher or student. Fill in your required information, including your email address and password. If you're a student, you may need a class code from your teacher to join a class. Once completed, you'll have immediate access to PABASA.
Yes, you can reset your password. On the login page, click the "Forgot Password?" link. Enter your registered email address, and we'll send you instructions to reset your password. Follow the link in the email and create a new password. If you don't receive the email, check your spam folder or contact our support team.
You can update your profile information by going to your account Settings. Click on your profile icon or avatar, select "Settings," and then choose "Edit Profile." You can update your name, email, profile picture, and other personal details. Remember to save your changes before leaving the page.
Yes, you can change your email address in your account Settings. Go to Settings, find the "Email Address" section, and enter your new email. You'll receive a verification email at your new address. Click the verification link to confirm the change. Your new email will then be associated with your PABASA account.
To start a reading session, log in to your PABASA account. If you're a teacher, navigate to the Courses section and select a course, then click "Start Reading Session." If you're a student, go to your dashboard and find the reading session assigned by your teacher. Click on it to begin reading the passage and follow the prompts.
Yes, PABASA is designed to work on phones, tablets, and desktop computers. Simply access PABASA through your device's web browser. For the best experience with oral reading assessments, we recommend using a device with a good microphone. The interface adapts to different screen sizes for easy navigation.
PABASA offers three different reading levels to help you practice progressively: Word level focuses on individual word recognition and pronunciation. Sentence level helps you read and understand complete thoughts. Paragraph level challenges you with longer, more complex passages. You can choose your level based on your current skill and comfort level.
To join a class, ask your teacher for the class code. Log into PABASA, go to "My Classes," and click "Join a Class." Enter the code your teacher provided and click "Join." You'll immediately have access to all course materials and assignments from your teacher.
PABASA automatically tracks your reading progress with each session you complete. The system records metrics like reading speed, accuracy, and fluency. Your progress dashboard shows improvement over time with detailed charts and summaries. Teachers can view your progress reports to identify areas where you're excelling and areas that need more practice.
Reading metrics include: Words Per Minute (WPM) - your reading speed; Accuracy - the percentage of words read correctly; Fluency - how naturally and smoothly you read; and Comprehension - your understanding of the material. Each metric helps identify your strengths and areas for improvement.
Yes, you can download your progress reports. Go to your Progress Dashboard, select the time period you'd like to review, and click "Download Report." Reports can be downloaded as PDF or Excel files for easy sharing with teachers, parents, or for your personal records.
The frequency of reading sessions depends on your teacher's assignments and your learning goals. Most students benefit from regular daily or weekly sessions. Consistent practice is key to improving reading skills. Your teacher will guide you on the recommended frequency for your class.
If you encounter a problem, first try refreshing the page or clearing your browser's cache. Make sure you're using a supported browser and that your internet connection is stable. If the issue persists, take note of what happened and contact our support team with detailed information about the problem.
You can reach our support team through the Help section in your PABASA account. There, you'll find contact information for different types of support, including technical assistance and account-related questions. You can also visit our About page to find contact information for specific team members who can assist with your inquiry.
If you can't access your account, ensure you're using the correct email and password. If you've forgotten your password, use the "Forgot Password?" link to reset it. If you're still unable to log in, try clearing your browser cache or using a different browser. If the problem continues, contact our support team for assistance.
We'd love to hear your feedback! You can provide suggestions and report issues through the Feedback section in your account menu. Select "Send Feedback" and describe your comments or concerns. Your feedback helps us improve PABASA and make it a better learning platform for everyone.
